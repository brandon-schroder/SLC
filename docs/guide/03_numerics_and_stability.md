## 1. Why SLC numerics are hard

The formulation (Guide 2) is a first-order ODE plus continuity — nothing
exotic. The difficulty is entirely in the **coupling**, and it has one
name: curvature.

The master equation's Tier-3 term is `Vm² κ_m cosε`, and `κ_m = dφ/dm` is a
*second* derivative of the streamline geometry (slope is the first). The
streamlines are fit through nodal positions that are themselves state
unknowns. So the loop is:

```
streamline positions  →  spline fit  →  κ_m (2nd derivative)
      ↑                                        │
      │                                        ▼
 mass distribution  ←  Vm(q) profile  ←  master ODE (κ_m feeds it)
```

Second derivatives amplify node-level noise viciously: a sub-percent wiggle
in a streamline position becomes a large wiggle in `κ_m`, which perturbs
`Vm`, which moves the streamline, which... The theory manual states it
flatly (§5.5): "Curvature is the noise amplifier of SLC." Essentially every
stabilization in the driver exists to keep this loop contractive. This
guide is the catalogue of those stabilizations and the measurements behind
each constant.

A second, quieter source of difficulty is that the master ODE's right-hand
side is `core/Vm` — **singular at `Vm = 0`**. Integrating from a boundary
value that is inconsistent with the current transported fields can walk the
profile across that singularity into negative-velocity nonsense. For five
milestones this was latent; in 2026-07 it turned out to be the real story
behind the "fragile radial Tier-3" reputation (§4).

## 2. The classical fixed point and its three couplings

Guide 1 §5 traced one outer iterate mechanically. Here is the same loop seen
as a **fixed-point iteration** with three coupled sub-problems, each with
its own stability constant:

| Coupling | Sub-problems | Stabilizer | Constant |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A. Position ↔ curvature** | repositioning moves streamlines; curvature feeds back through the ODE | Wilkinson relaxation `ω_sl` + curvature lag | `wilkinson_c = 4.4`, `kappa_relax = 0.3` |
| **B. Closure ↔ continuity** | swirl closure sets exit `rVθ`; continuity sets `Vm`; each shifts the other's input | closure under-relaxation | `closure_relax = 0.25` |
| **C. Field ↔ field (multistage)** | loss stratifies entropy spanwise; that reshapes the loss | spanwise mixing (physical, not numerical) | `c_mix = 5e-4` |

The three converge *together* — they are one coupled fixed point, which is
why the convergence records (Guide 1 §6) show positions and closures
contracting in lockstep. The rest of this section takes A and B in turn; C
(mixing) gets §5, because it is a physical model that happens to have
numerical consequences, not a numerical device.

### 2.1 Coupling A: the Wilkinson relaxation envelope (§6.4)

Repositioning moves each streamline only a fraction `ω_sl` of the way to
its mass-fraction target (`drivers/classical.py:596`, the `q_next = q_full +
ω_sl·(q_target − q_full)` blend). Too large and coupling A diverges; too
small and the solve crawls. The theory (Wilkinson 1970) gives the *scaling*
form

```
ω_sl ≲ C (1 − Mm²) (Δm/Δq)²        (literature aspect-ratio form)
```

but this implementation's measured envelope is **not** that form. The M3-3
calibration (`tools/calibrate_wilkinson.py`, Appendix C.3) swept fixed `ω`
on the V2 curved annulus at many grids and found the shipped form:

```
ω_sl ≤ K (1 − Mm²) (Δm_min / L_qo)^1.5      (implemented; classical.py:370)
```

Two deliberate departures from the textbook, each measured:

- **Exponent 3/2, not 2.** The fitted envelope matches `x^1.5` across the
  grid sweep (C.3 table), not `x²`.
- **Station density alone, not aspect ratio.** The threshold is *identical*
  for `n_sl = 5, 9, 17` at fixed station count — the instability is a
  *streamwise* odd-even mode of the per-q-o continuity solutions, so it
  depends on `Δm_min/L_qo` (streamwise spacing) and is blind to spanwise
  resolution. The literature `(Δm/Δq)²` aspect form was rejected because it
  *both* over-throttles coarse spanwise grids *and*, uncapped, licenses
  divergent factors on fine ones — both failure directions observed.

The measured threshold constant is `K ≈ 7.3`; the shipped default is
**`wilkinson_c = 4.4`**, a deliberate 0.6× margin, because the fit passes
within 2% of a *measured-unstable* point (the (9,19) grid) — margin is not
optional against a threshold you have touched. The C.3 sweep, abbreviated:

| Grid (N_sl, N_st) | Δm_min/L_qo | ω* measured | model 7.3·x^1.5 |
|---|---|---|---|
| (9, 5) | 0.262 | ≥ 0.70 (cap) | 0.98 |
| (9, 10) | 0.116 | 0.28 | 0.29 |
| (9, 13) | 0.087 | 0.20 | 0.19 |
| (9, 19) | 0.058 | < 0.10 (0.10 diverges) | 0.10 |

`ω_sl` is recomputed every iterate from the current worst-case `Δm_min/L_qo`
and peak `Mm`, then clipped to `[1e-3, omega_sl_max=0.7]`. When the
curvature term is off (Tiers 1–2), repositioning is a plain fixed point with
no curvature feedback, so it runs at the user cap 0.7 — the `curvature_on`
branch in `_omega_sl` is a tier/config decision, not a flow-value branch
(AD-1). Guide 1's run B showed `ω_sl` settling at 0.603, genuinely
throttled below the cap by station density.

**Headroom, and the multi-family recalibration (2026-07).** The envelope was
originally calibrated on the V2 *duct*. `tools/calibrate_wilkinson.py` has
since been extended with a `bladerow` family (the V7/V8 parametric bends,
run *after* the Tier-3 stabilization so blade-row divergences reflect the
genuine §6.4 mode rather than the driver artifact of §4). The finding: on
the blade-row bends the duct envelope is **2–3× over-conservative** — V8
Tier 3 reaches the *identical* fixed point at `wilkinson_c = 13.2` in ~152
iterations versus ~400 at the default, and only diverges near 22, so this
family's threshold constant sits around 8.8–30 rather than the duct's ~7.3.
**Yet the shipped default stays `wilkinson_c = 4.4`**: the recalibration
confirmed that the *duct* family is the binding constraint (a general-purpose
default must be safe on the stiffest geometry it may meet, and the margin
discipline is against the duct's calibrated ~7.3 threshold). The blade-row
headroom is exposed as a **per-case override** — safe up to ~13 on
V7/V8-class geometry — not baked into the default. This is the
recalibration that earlier drafts of this guide listed as an open item; it
is done, and its answer is "4.4 stands, with documented per-case headroom."

### 2.2 Coupling A: why the curvature lag is mandatory (§5.5)

`ω_sl` throttles how far streamlines *move*. Separately, the curvature that
enters the ODE is under-relaxed against the previous iterate — an
exponential moving average (`classical.py:544`, mirrored in the assembler's
`_build_dists`):

```
κ_used = kappa_relax · κ_new + (1 − kappa_relax) · κ_used_prev
```

with `kappa_relax = 0.3` whenever the curvature term is active. This is
**not** an optional smoother. The M3-1 measurement: without the lag, the
odd-even curvature mode diverges at *any* `ω_sl`, down to 0.02, on
station-dense curved paths. The `ω_sl` throttle governs the *position*
feedback; the κ-lag governs the *curvature-evaluation* feedback, and both
loops must be damped independently. The manual states it as a hard rule
(§6.4): "the lag is mandatory, not optional, whenever the curvature term is
active." The two together are why Tier 3 converges at all.

(There is a subtlety worth noting: the lag is *recursive* — it blends
against the previously *used* field, not the previous raw field, so the
driver replicates the assembler's blend to carry the EMA forward across
iterates. See `classical.py:546` and the matching `FrozenInputs.kappa_lagged`
plumbing.)

### 2.3 Coupling B: closure under-relaxation and the switch-on ramp

The swirl closure sets exit `rVθ` from the current LE flow; continuity then
resets `Vm`; that changes the LE flow the closure next sees. This Picard
loop's gain scales with `tan β₂` (highly staggered rows are the binding
case), and it **diverges at relaxation 0.5** on the coupled Lieblein rotor
(measured, M4). The shipped `closure_relax = 0.25` keeps margin
(`classical.py:630`):

```
rVθ_lagged ← rVθ_prev + 0.25·(rVθ_new − rVθ_prev)      (and likewise Δs)
```

This is the source of Picard's characteristic **linear tail**: the closure
norm contracts by a roughly constant factor ≈ 0.75 per iterate (0.25 ×
loop gain), so the flow field is visually converged long before the 10⁻⁹
gate is crossed. Guide 1's run A shows it — the answer is settled by
iterate ~10 but the norm needs 73 iterates to cross tolerance. Newton (§4)
exists precisely to replace this tail with quadratic steps.

**The first-application ramp (2026-07).** Rows join the solve at iterate 2
(iterate 1 is duct-only, since closures need a flow field to evaluate
against). Originally that first application was *unrelaxed*: a one-iterate
jump from duct baseline (swept LE `rVθ`, zero loss) straight to
full-strength closure output. That violent transport change was the main
*producer* of the stale boundary values behind the Tier-3 failures (§4).
The fix: the first application relaxes from the duct baseline through the
*same* §6.2.4 rule as every later one (`classical.py:631`, the `else` branch
that seeds `prev_rvt`/`prev_ds` from the duct state). This gentler ramp-in
is why run A now takes 73 iterates instead of 63 — a deliberate trade of a
few iterations for a much larger stability basin.

## 3. The elimination and the residual — what Newton differentiates

Before the stabilization story, one structural point that makes everything
else possible. The state vector is small because momentum is *eliminated*,
not solved (Guide 1 §D.3, §6.1): integrating the master ODE from `Vm(q=0)`
produces the whole `Vm(q)` profile, so momentum is satisfied by
construction and contributes no residual rows. What remains
(`assembler.py:342` `residual`) is:

- `n_qo` continuity rows `R_cont_j` (one per station), and
- interior streamtube mass-fraction rows `R_pos_ij`,
- plus, in back-pressure mode, one throttling-station pressure row.

This residual is a **pure function of `(x, FrozenInputs)`** (AD-3) — no
mutation, no I/O, no hidden state. That purity is not an aesthetic
preference: it is the precondition that lets the Newton driver
finite-difference the residual safely (§4.4), and it is why closures are
lagged into frozen data rather than called on the residual path (AD-4).
Break the purity and the Jacobian desynchronizes from the residual.

## 4. The 2026-07 Tier-3 stabilization — a diagnosis case study

This is the centerpiece of the dossier, and it is as much a lesson in
*diagnosis discipline* as in numerics. For five milestones the project
carried a documented "known limitation": Tier-3 radial/mixed repositioning
was *fragile* — V7 (90° bend) converged only in a narrow `(n_sl, n_inblade)`
pocket, V8 (55° bend) converged *nowhere*, and the story attributed it to
"the §6.4 odd-even repositioning mode," an angle-specific stability envelope.
It was the top item on every open-items list, including this series' plan.

**It was wrong.** Not the observation — the failures were real — but the
*attribution*. A diagnosis-first session (scratchpad probes `diag_v8_*.py`)
overturned it.

### 4.1 The evidence that killed the "odd-even mode" story

Three measurements, each contradicting the repositioning-instability
attribution:

1. **Streamlines barely move before death.** `pos_norm ≈ 2e-4` at the
   iterate the solve dies. An odd-even *repositioning* mode would show
   streamlines thrashing; they are nearly still.
2. **Pure repositioning-with-curvature on the same bend is stable.** Run the
   identical 55° geometry as a *duct* (no blade row) at Tier 3 — repositioning
   and curvature both active — and it converges fine.
3. **Density is irrelevant.** The failure does not track `n_sl` or
   `n_inblade` the way a discretization-stability envelope must.

So whatever was killing V7/V8 Tier 3, it was not the repositioning loop.

### 4.2 The actual root-cause chain

Every step below is evidenced (memory: `v8-tier3-root-cause`):

1. **Trigger — a stale boundary value.** `Vm(q=0)` for a station is, at the
   top of each iterate, still the *previous* iterate's value (the per-q-o
   solves that will replace it run later). After a large transport change —
   the §6.2.1 cold 1-D guess, or (chiefly) the **unrelaxed closure switch-on
   at iterate 2**, a duct→full-swirl jump in one step — that stale value is
   far from continuity-consistent with the new transported fields.
2. **Amplifier — why radial/mixed specifically.** Mid-bend q-o's sit at low
   radius with high `rVθ` and steep spanwise swirl gradients. The REE swirl
   term `−(rVθ/r²)·d(rVθ)/dq` reaches ≈ −2000·Vm per metre there — an
   order of magnitude stiffer than on an axial q-o. Axial machines never hit
   this because the amplifier is absent.
3. **Singularity — the `Vm = 0` cliff.** The master ODE's RHS is `core/Vm`.
   Integrating from an inconsistent `Vm(q=0)` through that stiff term drives
   the profile across `Vm = 0` into a spurious negative-velocity branch:
   `|Vm| ~ 1e4`, static enthalpy `h < 0`, and the fluid returns NaN. There
   was no domain guard in `_integrate`/`split` — an AD-10 gap.
4. **Kill shot — the boundary check fired on a repairable state.** The
   driver boundary-checked the *full split at the stale `Vm(q=0)`* — **before**
   the per-q-o continuity solves that exist to repair it. Proven recoverable:
   a cold `_solve_qo` on the *exact* FrozenInputs the driver had just
   declared a NUMERICAL_FAILURE succeeds on every q-o (`max|F|/ṁ = 4e-15`,
   `Mm = 0.53`). The driver was killing states it could solve.
5. **Secondary corruptions** when the garbage stayed finite instead of NaN:
   `ω_sl` computed from garbage Mach floored at 1e-3 (freezing the solve);
   the warm-bracket `brentq` with the `NaN→−1e30` mapping converged on the
   domain-*cliff* pseudo-root; and `_solve_qo` accepted roots with **negative
   interior Vm** where `F = 0` held by sign cancellation in the mass integral
   — which is *exactly* how V7 edge-only died ("cumulative integral is
   decreasing").

The punchline: **the "narrow pocket" and "angle-specificity" were chaos in
whether the transient garbage happened to stay finite or tip into NaN** —
not a stability envelope at all. Tier 2 always survived only because,
without the curvature amplification, the garbage stayed finite and the
per-q-o solves repaired it each iterate.

### 4.3 The four fixes

All in `drivers/classical.py` plus a capacity guard in `assembler.py`:

1. **Move the AD-10 flow-field check to the *solved* state.** The stale-guess
   split is now wrapped in `errstate` and only its *geometry* (metrics,
   interpolants) is consumed — NaN in its flow fields is expected and
   recoverable. The fatal boundary check moves *after* the per-q-o solves
   (`classical.py:580`). A broken *metrics* fit stays fatal
   (`classical.py:537`) — that is genuinely unrecoverable.
2. **Validate continuity roots onto the positive branch** (`_solve_qo`,
   `classical.py:322` `rooted`). Every bracket the pre-guard code would have
   tried is *still* tried — but the returned root's own integrated profile
   must be strictly positive, finite, and its `|F| < 1%·ṁ` a genuine zero.
   Cliff pseudo-roots and negative-interior-Vm roots are rejected, falling
   through to the next candidate. **Critically, this is root validation, not
   endpoint vetting**: vetting bracket *endpoints* was measured to change the
   happy-path dynamics and destabilize the V7 closure-lag trajectory into a
   growing oscillation ending in false choke. The old code's legitimate roots
   all pass unchanged.
3. **Freeze-and-patience for transient deficiency** (`classical.py:558`). A
   q-o with no positive-branch root *this* iterate keeps its previous
   boundary value (no kick into the repositioning targets) and lets the
   lagged fields relax. `CHOKE_LIMITED` is declared only after
   `choke_patience = 15` *consecutive* deficient iterations — genuine choke
   persists indefinitely, while the switch-on transient clears within the
   closure ramp-in window (≈ 2/closure_relax, measured 8 on V8).
4. **Relax the first closure application** from the duct baseline (§2.3
   above) — removing the main transient producer at the source.

The Newton path got the matching guard in the consolidation sprint:
`_safe_residual` (`newton.py:179`) now splits once, rejects any trial whose
integrated `Vm` is not strictly positive, and evaluates the rows through the
new `residual_from(fields, x)` seam. The measured motivation: spurious
negative-Vm branches carry *finite* residuals (mass balancing by
cancellation) even on plain V1c, so they passed the old feasibility screen
and could poison the line-search merit.

### 4.4 What the fix refuted

Three of the project's own recorded claims fell with the artifact. This is
the honest part — the guide series must record what turned out to be wrong:

| Claim (pre-2026-07) | Reality after diagnosis |
|---|---|
| V8 Tier-3 "converges nowhere; angle-specific pocket" | Converges: 396 iterations, PR 1.5873, within a few % of Tier 2 (`test_v8_mixed_flow.py::test_tier3_converges_after_stabilization`) |
| V7 Tier-3 "needs 6 INBLADE stations; edge-only diverges" | Edge-only converges: 173 iterations, PR 2.4433 vs the subdivided 2.4540 (<0.5%). **INBLADE is a resolution choice, not a convergence requirement.** |
| Multistage mixing is a "convergence prerequisite" | Mixing-off *converges* (92 iterations, PR 1.1973). The surviving claim is physical stratification (§5), not convergence. |

Two in-suite tripwires that used to assert *divergence* were flipped to
assert *convergence*, so a regression that reintroduces the artifact fails
loudly.

**The remaining honest limitation is speed, not fragility.** V8 Tier 3
converges but slowly — the §6.4 throttle holds `ω_sl ≈ 0.066` on that bend,
hence ~400 iterations. The headroom probe (§2.1) and Newton finishing (§4.5)
are the routes to accelerating it. And the *principled* fix for the
singularity itself — integrating `ln Vm` so positivity is structural rather
than screened — is recorded for the day a new case resurfaces it.

## 4.5 Newton: escaping Picard's linear tail

The classical driver's linear tail (§2.3) is its intrinsic cost. The Newton
driver (`drivers/newton.py`, §6.3) solves the *same* pure residual
simultaneously and converges quadratically. Measured on V1c (forced vortex,
`n_sl = 17`) from a deliberately partial 3-iterate warm start:

```
Newton record 1: scaled |r| = 2.81e-08   α = 1.0
Newton record 2: scaled |r| = 2.23e-15   α = 1.0
Newton record 3: scaled |r| = 0.0        α = 1.0
max |x_newton − x_classical| = 7.06e-09     (lands on the same solution)
```

Correct digits roughly double each step, full Newton step (`α = 1`)
throughout — textbook quadratic, versus 15 classical iterations to the same
answer. Four design points:

- **Warm start is mandatory** (ARCH-5.3). Newton is local; the seed is a
  classical iterate or a neighbouring converged operating point. `solve_newton`
  raises `ConfigError` without one.
- **Colored forward-difference Jacobian** (the default since 2026-07;
  `jacobian="dense"` keeps the dense version as the validation oracle and the
  automatic fallback). The coloring is **exact by construction** — it groups
  only *provably-disjoint* columns, so it matches the dense Jacobian
  column-for-column to FD noise in every configuration, never approximating.
  The measured structure is more subtle than the arch spec's
  "near-block-tridiagonal" guess: the `Vm(q=0)` columns are exactly
  block-diagonal in station index at every tier (one color for all of them —
  the lean term reads the *lagged* Vm, so no cross-station Vm coupling), but
  the interior-`q` columns are block-diagonal only when the curvature/lean
  terms are off *and* the annulus is straight (`|sinε| ≈ 0`); on curved paths
  the coupling is first-order and under active curvature the interpolating
  spline couples stations *globally* (~0.27/station decay). Net payoff:
  straight-annulus Tier-2/meanline solves — the continuation/back-pressure
  workhorse — get `(n_sl−1)`-color Jacobians (measured 3.7× on V1c); curved
  and Tier-3 cases keep per-column `q` plus the one free `Vm` color (~15% on
  V8). A banded stride-6 approximation was measured *unprofitable* end-to-end
  (aliasing inflated inner iterations ~1.7×, more than the Jacobian saved)
  and deliberately does not ship.
- **Two globalization guards fold into the Armijo line search.** A trial that
  makes a q-o's nodes non-monotone (a *crossing streamline*, which PCHIP
  cannot represent) gets merit `+∞` and backtracks (`_is_feasible_q`,
  inspected *without* building the interpolants that would raise). And the
  positive-branch guard from §4.3 rejects negative-Vm trials with finite
  residuals. Neither raises — AD-10 holds on the Newton path.
- **Closures stay lagged** in an outer quasi-Newton loop; within one inner
  solve the `FrozenInputs` are fixed, so the inner system is exactly the
  frozen-closure residual (and at Tier 2, the exact full system).

**Why Newton does not yet *finish* the hard cases.** You would expect to
escalate to Newton for the slow V8 Tier-3 tail. Measured, it is
*unprofitable*: the inner Newton is textbook (2–3 quadratic iterations per
pass), but the quasi-Newton *closure* outer loop still contracts at only
~0.73/pass after a several-pass hump, so ~50+ Jacobian-bearing passes are
needed — and colored-FD, which rescued the *straight* cases (3.7×), buys
only ~15% on exactly this curved Tier-3 geometry (the coloring degrades to
per-column `q` when curvature couples the stations globally, above). So the
one lever that would have made Newton-finishing pay does not pay *here*. The
remaining profitability gate is **closure-in-Newton** (folding the lagged
closures into the simultaneous solve, collapsing the outer loop that is
actually the bottleneck). Until then, continuation escalates classical→Newton
only for *point* failures, not to finish slow-but-converging tails
(`continuation.py:269`). A measured decision, recorded so it is not
re-litigated blindly.

## 5. The mixing operator (§3.6)

Spanwise mixing is coupling C — but unlike A and B it is a *physical* model
with numerical side effects, not a numerical stabilizer. Multistage machines
develop real turbulent redistribution of `h0`, `s`, `rVθ` across the span;
without a model for it, the solved entropy stratification is unphysically
large. The operator (`transport/mixing.py`) is an implicit diffusion applied
in the lagged refresh (never the residual path, AD-4):

```
∂χ/∂m = 1/(r(1−B)ρVm) · ∂/∂q ( μ_mix · r · ∂χ/∂q ),   χ ∈ {h0, s, rVθ}
```

Three properties, each a deliberate numerical choice:

- **Implicit (backward-Euler in m, tridiagonal in q).** Unconditionally
  stable — the marching step is never limited by the mixing coefficient. A
  stiff `μ_mix` cannot blow up the step.
- **Finite-volume with zero-flux walls ⇒ conservative.** The step conserves
  the mass-flux-weighted total `Σ wᵢ dqᵢ χᵢ` exactly (`w = r(1−B)ρVm`, the
  §3.2 weight). Mixing *redistributes* h0/s/rVθ; it does not create or
  destroy their fluxes. This is a machine-precision invariant, tested.
- **A strict no-op unless opted in.** `strength = FidelityConfig.mixing_term`
  gates it; 0 returns the input untouched, so the V3 Tier-2 ≡ Tier-3 identity
  is never disturbed by a mixing model being *present*.

**What the multistage measurement actually shows (revised three times — a
cautionary tale).** Guide 1 originally reported mixing as a *convergence
prerequisite* — that the un-mixed two-stage compressor "ran away and failed
outright." The 2026-07 stabilization (§4) showed that non-convergence was the
driver artifact, not physics: post-fix the un-mixed case **converges
cleanly**. That refutation is stable. The *quantitative* benefit was then
reported as *dramatic* (~25×, then ~6× as the Lieblein loss was corrected) —
but that too was an artifact, of two compounding calibration errors. At the
honest coefficient it is **modest**:

| Multistage V5, n_sl=9, Tier 3 — snapshot @ `13e8978` | Converged | validity | exit Δs spread |
|---|---|---|---|
| mixing **off** | yes | **0.0** | 1.88 J/(kg·K) |
| mixing **on** (Gallimore, c_mix=5e-4) | yes | **0.0** | 1.68 J/(kg·K) |

The reduction is ~1.1× (~11%), and it does *not* catch up as stratification
grows (off→on: 3 stages 6.83→6.29 = 8%, 4 stages 10.32→9.42 = 9%). Read the
history as a warning: the ratio was ~25× when the compressor loss was ~4× too
high (the Lieblein ω̄ inversion) **and** `c_mix` was ~20× too strong
(`0.01`); ~6× after the ω̄ fix alone; ~1.1× now that `c_mix` is
Gallimore–Cumpsty-calibrated (`5e-4`, §5 below / `docs/references/GC86.md`).
The "mixing flattens multistage stratification" narrative does **not** survive
an honest coefficient on this case. One caveat keeps the absolute numbers
soft: **both runs sit at validity 0** — but that is a *family-wide* property
(the representative V5 metal angles sit outside the Lieblein calibration
window, so *every* V5 case runs saturated; even the single-stage rotor reads
validity 0), a standing `[VERIFY]`, not something unique to this case.

What survives is **structural**, and it is what the guide should lean on:
mixing is provably conservative (machine-precision, tested), it reduces
spanwise stratification *by construction* (the direction is guaranteed; the
*smallness* is the measured finding), and the un-mixed solve converges. `c_mix`
is now G–C-calibrated (`5e-4`; the code non-dimensionalizes on radius not
stage length, so the constant carries an implicit `L_s/r` factor — the
accepted cost of the r-based retune, GC86.md option B); the mixing
entropy-*production* term is a recorded refinement. The normative Appendix
C.5m now carries this ~11% reality.

## 6. Continuation and BC switching (§6.6–6.7)

The continuation driver (`drivers/continuation.py`) is more orchestration
than measured-finding, so briefly. `solve_speedline` traverses a speedline by
natural-parameter continuation in mass flow (choke→stall), each point
warm-started from its converged neighbour, with cut-back on failure and
classical→Newton escalation before a step is rejected. It reports operability
rather than solving through stall (§6.7): a point is flagged with the *exact*
criterion that fired — `solver_failure`, `pr_turnover` (PR falling at
falling ṁ, but only after an "arm" threshold so a still-rising or flat
characteristic never false-flags), or `validity_saturated`.

The one genuinely subtle piece is the **hysteretic BC switch** (§6.6). Near
choke the mass-flow-specified form's continuity Jacobian goes singular (the
capacity peak, A.7), so the driver switches the operating-point spec to
back-pressure — `mdot` joins the state, the assembler appends one exit-pressure
residual — and switches *back* only once the choke margin recovers past
`c_sw + δ_hys`. The hysteresis band is what prevents limit-cycling at the
boundary. The mode decision (`_next_mode`) is a pure function precisely so
the "automatic and hysteretic" requirement is unit-testable in isolation.

## 8. Check your understanding

1. **Why is curvature, specifically, the thing every stabilizer targets?**
   Because `κ_m` is a *second* derivative of streamline geometry, and the
   streamlines are fit through state-unknown positions — second derivatives
   amplify node noise, closing a feedback loop (position → κ_m → Vm →
   position) that is only contractive when both the position move (`ω_sl`)
   and the curvature evaluation (`kappa_relax`) are damped (§1, §2.1–2.2).
2. **This implementation's `ω_sl` uses `(Δm_min/L_qo)^1.5`, not Wilkinson's
   `(Δm/Δq)²`. Give one reason the aspect form was rejected.** It depends on
   spanwise resolution `Δq`, but the measured mode depends on station density
   alone (identical thresholds for n_sl 5/9/17); the aspect form over-throttles
   coarse spanwise grids and, uncapped, licenses divergent factors on fine
   ones (§2.1).
3. **The classical closure norm contracts at ~0.75/iterate no matter how good
   the guess. Why, and what removes it?** It is the fixed-point contraction
   rate of coupling B — `closure_relax` (0.25) times the physical loop gain —
   an intrinsic linear tail. Newton replaces it with quadratic steps (§2.3,
   §4.5).
4. **For five milestones V8 Tier 3 "converged nowhere." What was actually
   wrong, and why did it masquerade as a repositioning-stability envelope?**
   The master ODE's `Vm = 0` singularity, reached by integrating from a stale
   boundary value (chiefly the unrelaxed closure switch-on) amplified by the
   low-radius swirl term — combined with a boundary check that killed the
   repairable stale state and a root finder that accepted spurious
   negative-Vm roots. It looked angle-/density-specific because the real
   variable was whether the transient garbage stayed finite or tipped to NaN
   — chaos, not an envelope (§4.1–4.2).
5. **The V7 edge-only case now converges. What claim did that refute, and
   what is the standing role of INBLADE stations?** It refuted "Tier-3 radial
   repositioning needs INBLADE subdivision." INBLADE stations remain the
   *resolution* choice for distributing in-blade work and loss (§2.5, §3.4) —
   they are not a convergence requirement (§4.4).
6. **Newton converges V8 Tier-3's inner system in 2–3 iterations. Why doesn't
   the driver escalate to Newton to finish that case?** Because the
   *quasi-Newton closure outer* loop still contracts at only ~0.73/pass, so
   ~50+ Jacobian-bearing passes are needed — and the colored-FD Jacobian that
   rescued the *straight* cases (3.7×) buys only ~15% on this *curved* Tier-3
   geometry, where curvature couples the stations globally and the coloring
   degrades to per-column `q`. The lever that would have paid does not pay
   here; the remaining gate is closure-in-Newton (§4.5).
7. **Mixing is now known *not* to be a convergence prerequisite, *and* not a
   dramatic homogenizer. What is the surviving, tested claim for why multistage
   machines need it?** That it is conservative (machine-precision) and reduces
   spanwise entropy stratification *by construction* (the *direction* is
   guaranteed). At the Gallimore–Cumpsty-calibrated `c_mix = 5e-4` the *size*
   of the reduction is **modest** — ~11% on two stages, not catching up as
   stratification grows (§5). The historical ratio (~25× → ~6× → ~1.1×) is
   the story of two compounding calibration errors (the Lieblein ω̄ inversion
   and a ~20×-strong `c_mix`) being removed one at a time — a cautionary
   example of an inflated claim, not a moving physical target.
