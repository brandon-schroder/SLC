# TR-1368 — NACA 65-series cascade data (Herrig, Emery & Erwin)

**Source:** NACA RM L51G31, *Systematic Two-Dimensional Cascade Tests of NACA
65-Series Compressor Blades at Low Speeds* (1951), later published as NACA
TR-1368. Public domain, NTRS **19930092353**
(<https://ntrs.nasa.gov/api/citations/19930092353/downloads/19930092353.pdf>,
116 MB scan, not vendored). Also a source in the "Turbomachinery: Test Cases"
NotebookLM notebook (2026-07-15).

**Role in the validation ladder.** This is the *primary measured data* the
whole axial-compressor closure chain rests on: TR-1368 raw cascade data →
Lieblein's SP-36 minimum-loss reduction (charts) → Aungier's analytic fits →
`closures/axial_compressor/lieblein.py`. The SP-36 and Aungier layers were
validated in the reference-calibration pass (`AUN-C.md`, `LIEB59.md`,
`tools/digitize_sp36.py`); this note records the first check against the
**raw-data layer itself** (`model-readiness` gate #1, V4 cascade level).

## What the report provides

- Test matrix (Table III): β₁ ∈ {30, 45, 60, 70}°, σ ∈ {0.50, 0.75, 1.00,
  1.25, 1.50}, blades 65-010 … 65-(27)10 (c_l0 0 … 2.7), 10% thickness.
- Per-configuration measured θ vs α + surface pressures + drag (Figs 31–106).
- Summary charts: design θ_d and α_d vs c_l0 per (β₁, σ) (Fig. 111 a–e,
  pp. 219–223 of the report) and the design-point turning slope
  **(dθ/dα)_d vs σ per β₁ (Fig. 107, p. 214)**.
- Blade geometry: a=1.0 mean line ordinates/slopes (Table II) — max camber
  ordinate **0.05515·c·c_l0** at 50% chord (used below).

## 65-series ↔ equivalent-circular-arc conversion (grounded)

Lieblein's reduction (SP-36 ch. VI; confirmed via the loss-models notebook,
2026-07-15) replaces the 65-series a=1.0 mean line by the circular arc with
the **same chord and the same maximum camber**; angle of attack maps to
incidence as **i = α − θ/2** (LE tangent of an arc is θ/2 off the chord).
With max camber h/c = 0.05515·c_l0 (Table II) and the exact arc identity
tan(θ/4) = 2h/c:

    θ_eq = 4·arctan(0.11030 · c_l0)      (e.g. c_l0 = 1.2 → θ_eq = 30.2°)

Every ingredient is source-grounded (notebook statement + on-disk Table II +
arc geometry); no fitted constant is involved.

## Fig. 107 digitization → `deviation_slope` validation (DONE 2026-07-15)

With fixed geometry, (dθ/dα)_d = 1 − (dδ/di), and dδ/di is exactly
`lieblein.deviation_slope` (§4.3) — so Fig. 107 validates that fit directly
at the raw-data level, with **no camber-equivalence step**.

Method: 300-dpi render; calibration anchored to the printed uniform grid
(the digitize_sp36 lesson — never ink-weight frame detection); seeded
bidirectional continuity tracing (gridlines + label glyphs rejected);
**decisive check = overlay** of the traced points on the chart (all three
curves ride the print over their full span). Reading precision ±0.01.
Rerunnable: `tools/digitize_tr1368_fig107.py`; pinned:
`tests/test_lieblein_tr1368_fig107.py`.

**Findings** (39 points): RMS **0.030**, mean +0.002.

| family | agreement |
|--------|-----------|
| β₁=30 | \|err\| ≤ 0.013 — excellent |
| β₁=45 | ~0.02–0.03 below the chart's single faired 30/45 curve (code's 30↔45 spread ≈ the chart's own fairing width) |
| β₁=60 | +0.04…+0.06 at σ ≤ 0.8, tapering to −0.025 at σ = 1.5 |
| β₁=70 | **documented deviation region**: fit σ-dependence shallower than measured; +0.097 at σ = 1.0 → −0.039 at σ = 1.5 |

The β₁ = 70 corner is the edge of the fits' calibrated domain
(`CALIBRATED["beta1_deg"]` hi = 70) and the most extreme cascade condition
tested; the chart itself is "averages for the moderate camber range", and
NACA's design point (smooth pressure distribution) is not identically the
SP-36 minimum-loss reference Aungier fit. Recorded, bounded by test, not
tuned away.
