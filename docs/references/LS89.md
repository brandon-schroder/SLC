# LS89 — VKI LS-89 transonic turbine nozzle guide vane (Arts et al.)

**Source:** Arts & Lambert de Rouvroit, *Aero-Thermal Performance of a
Two-Dimensional Highly Loaded Transonic Turbine Nozzle Guide Vane* (VKI
TN-174 / J. Turbomach. 1992, DOI 10.1115/1.2927978). In the
"Turbomachinery: Test Cases" NotebookLM notebook; values below extracted
verbatim via the notebook (2026-07-15).

**Role:** the standard transonic HP-turbine cascade — the V6 cascade-level
measured-data check of the Ainley exit-angle rule and the K-O loss chain
(`model-readiness` gate #1; the turbine analogue of the TR-1368 pass).

## Extracted values

- Chord 67.647 mm; pitch/chord g/c = 0.850; stagger 55.0° (from axial);
  throat/pitch **o/g = 0.2597**; TE radius r_TE/c = 0.0105
  (t_TE = 2·r_TE → t_TE/o = 0.0951); axial inflow (α1 = 0).
- Loss = **energy loss coefficient** ζ₂ = 1 − V₂²/V₂,is² (exact
  compressible p-ratio form quoted in the paper).
- **Measured at M2is = 1.0:** total ζ₂ ≈ **2.25%**, decomposed ≈ 1.0%
  boundary-layer + 0.75% trailing-edge + 0.5% shock + ~0 base pressure.
  Re₂,is 0.5–2.0×10⁶, loss insensitive to Re in that range. The
  loss-vs-M2is characteristic (M2 0.7–1.1) is Fig. 16 — a figure, not
  tabulated; exact per-Mach values "available upon request".
- Exit flow angle ≈ the gauging angle sin⁻¹(o/g) = 15.05° from tangential
  (= 74.95° from axial); detailed per-Mach angle calibration not tabulated.

## Comparison vs the coded closures (pinned: `tests/test_v6_ls89.py`)

Closure-level (2-D stationary cascade — pure K-O functions mirroring
`KackerOkapuuLoss.evaluate` for a nozzle at midspan; no machine solve):

- **Exit angle:** `throat_exit_angle(0.2597)` = 74.95° — coincides with the
  paper's gauging angle to 0.1° (the arccos(o/s) M2=1 gauge rule evaluated
  exactly at its design condition, M2is = 1.0).
- **Total loss:** predicted Y 0.0464 → energy-ζ **0.0303 vs measured
  0.0225 (+35%)**. K-O over-predicting a modern high-efficiency vane by
  30–50% is the documented behaviour of the 1982 mean-line method
  (calibrated on older hardware, ±1.5% stage-η target). Band pinned
  [0.7×, 1.7×] — catches a regression to the pre-calibration constants
  (~3× high) or a reference-frame error.
- **TE component:** K-O ζ_TE(t/o=0.095) ≈ 1.9% energy-coefficient (→ ζ ≈
  1.3% at M2=1) vs the rig's measured TE share ~0.75% (calibrated on
  thicker-TE hardware); recorded, bounded in test. **But the TE is NOT the
  dominant over-prediction — see the calibration disposition below.**
- **Shock:** the K-O shock term is an *inlet* (LE) component and correctly
  reads ~0 at M1 ≈ 0.15; the measured 0.5% is an *exit/TE* shock system the
  method does not model separately at M2 ≤ 1 — recorded model boundary.

## Calibration disposition — K-O TE curve (2026-07-18, gate #2)

Investigated per "K-O TE curve calibration"; the disciplined outcome is a
**disposition, not a curve change** (grounded via the loss-models notebook:
Kacker-Okapuu 1982, Zhu & Sjolander 2005, Liu et al. 2022, Denton 1993):

1. **Convention CONFIRMED correct.** `t_TE` is the full trailing-edge
   thickness (= 2·r_TE for a rounded TE, "normal to camber at TE" per AM
   1951) and the denominator is the throat opening `o` — exactly the coded
   `t_TE/o = 2·r_TE/(o/g·g) = 0.095`. No convention bug.
2. **The K-O TE curve is faithful** to KO82 Fig. 14 (validated 2026-07-10,
   `tools/digitize_ko82_fig14.py`). The over-prediction is the correlation's,
   not a fit error.
3. **The +35% is a TOTAL over-prediction; the TE is not the majority.**
   Decomposed at M2is=1 (energy-ζ): profile-only ≈ 0.018, TE-only ≈ 0.013,
   plus the missing exit-shock ≈ 0.005 — the TE (~1.3%) is not the bulk of
   the ~0.030 predicted total, so a TE-curve tweak cannot close the gap (the
   earlier "the TE curve carries most of it" reading is **corrected**). The
   *robust, apples-to-apples* statement is the **+35% total**; the
   profile-vs-BL component split is informative but not clean (K-O's profile
   bucket = surface BL + wake, which the paper distributes across its 1.0% BL
   and part of the 0.75% TE). Pinned:
   `test_v6_ls89.py::test_component_decomposition_is_not_te_dominated`.
4. **No grounded correlation fix is available — including Zhu-Sjolander
   (REFUTED here, 2026-07-18):**
   - *Zhu & Sjolander 2005* (GT2005-69077, grounded via the loss-models
     notebook) — **would NOT fix LS-89, and would make it worse.** Their
     Eq. 13 `Y_p = 0.914(k_in·Y'_P,AM·k_p + Y_shock)(Re/2e5)^K_R + Y'_TET`
     and Eq. 12 use the **same Ainley-Mathieson reference curves** (their
     Fig. 1, "originally from Ainley and Mathieson" = our `yp1`/`yp2`) and
     leave **K-O's TE loss unchanged** (`Y'_TET` "as in the K-O system"). The
     profile changes are `k_in` (2/3 reaction → **0.825 axial-entry nozzle**),
     a thickness exponent `K_m` (+1 for t/c≤0.2, −1 for t/c>0.2, replacing
     K-O's `b1/b2`), and `K_R = −0.575` for Re<2e5 (inert at LS-89's Re=1e6).
     For an axial-entry nozzle `k_in` **rises +24%** — ZS found K-O
     *under*-predicts nozzles, the OPPOSITE direction to this vane's
     over-prediction. The only reducing lever is `(t_max/c/0.2)^K_m`, ~neutral
     at LS-89's t/c≈0.2; net ZS/K-O profile is ~+24% (worse) at t/c 0.2 and
     only −7% at a thin t/c 0.15 — nowhere near the ~−50% needed. ZS targets
     large-s/c (>1) / large-thickness / low-Re and corrects *under*-predicted
     nozzles upward; it is not the modern-high-efficiency-vane fix. Pinned:
     `test_v6_ls89.py::test_zhu_sjolander_would_not_fix_ls89`. (Equations
     captured above for a future large-s/c or low-Re case, where ZS *is* the
     right variant — but no case in the set needs it today.)
   - *Liu et al. 2022* — enhance the TE with `Y_te,Enh = K_M·K_p·K_Re·Y_te,KO`;
     the K_p·K_Re part is grounded but **~a no-op at LS-89** (M2=1, Re=1e6 →
     ×0.978) while it **perturbs the ~1%-validated TN D-6967** (×0.87 at
     moderate M2); the dominant near-sonic `K_M` factor's formula is **not in
     the library**. Declined.
   - *Denton 1993* — physical base-pressure TE loss `Y = 2θ/w + ((δ*+t)/w)² −
     C_Pb·t/w`; needs a base-pressure coefficient `C_Pb` not in the §4.1
     geometry contract.

   **Disposition: the +35% is accepted documented K-O conservatism on a
   modern high-efficiency vane; no grounded correlation swap fixes it.**
   Constants unchanged; local single-point tuning declined (discipline). The
   bands in `test_total_loss_vs_measured_ls89_band` keep drift visible.
